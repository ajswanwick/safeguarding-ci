What Are The Signs

Some signs that someone may be experiencing abuse, neglect, or exploitation include:
Physical abuse: Unexplained injuries, bruising, burns, fractures, or lacerations, especially in non-accidental injury sites like the tops of the arms or neck
Psychological abuse: Fearfulness, low self-esteem, ambivalence about the carer, or sudden speech disorders
Financial abuse: Withholding money or inappropriate use of money
Discriminatory abuse: Hate mail, verbal or physical abuse, or criminal damage
Emotional abuse: Lack of empathy, self-harm, or eating disorders
hygiene: Dirty or unhygienic environment, poor personal hygiene, or pressure sores
physical condition: Malnutrition, unexplained weight loss, or changes in weight
medical issues: Untreated injuries or medical problems
clothing: Inappropriate or inadequate clothing
behavior: Sudden, unexplainable changes in attitude or behavior
Children or young people may disclose their experiences of abuse directly or indirectly
If you notice signs of abuse or neglect, you can report your concerns to the authorities or call 999
